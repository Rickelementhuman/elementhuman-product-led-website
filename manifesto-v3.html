ELEMENT HUMAN

The next frontier of AI is not intelligence. It’s understanding.

Language models can reason, create, and communicate at scale. What they cannot do is know how you feel. That gap — between what people express and what they experience — is where AI breaks down. In every interaction. At every scale.

We are building the emotional world model for AI: a new class of system that understands human psychological state, builds persistent memory of what matters to people, and acts always in their best interest — safely, privately, and under their control.

This is not a feature. It is a new layer of intelligence — one the entire AI stack is currently missing.

Thirteen years of behavioural science and over 30 billion real human moments of attention, emotion, and memory sit at our foundation. That dataset, and the models trained on it, are the beginning of something we believe will sit alongside every AI system in the world.

We are building this with researchers, engineers, and partners who share one conviction: that empathy is not soft. It is structural. And building it responsibly — with safety and human control at the centre — is the most important infrastructure problem in AI today.
